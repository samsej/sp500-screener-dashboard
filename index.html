How to read this dashboard
Opportunity Score (0–100)
A single score that combines financial quality, price momentum, and risk to rank how attractive each stock looks today. 70+ is strong; below 40 is generally not worth pursuing. Stocks flagged as high-risk automatically score 0.
Quality
How financially healthy the company is, based on earnings, profit margins, debt, and growth. GREAT = strong fundamentals; GOOD = solid; OK = average; WEAK = concerns that could amplify downside.
EPS Growth %
Year-over-year change in earnings per share. Green = growing profits; red = declining. One bad year doesn't define a company — look alongside Quality and the long-term trend.
Revenue Growth %
Year-over-year change in total revenue. Sustained growth signals healthy business expansion. Declining revenue (red) suggests the business is shrinking or under pricing pressure.
ROE %
Return on Equity — how much profit the company generates per dollar of shareholder equity. Higher is generally better. Values above 100% can occur when share buybacks reduce the equity base; interpret in sector context.
Debt / Equity
Total debt relative to shareholder equity. Lower means less financial leverage. High ratios are normal in some sectors (financials, utilities) but can signal meaningful risk elsewhere, especially if earnings are declining.
Forward P/E
Stock price relative to expected next-year earnings. A lower number can indicate the stock is cheaper relative to its expected profit. Very high values suggest growth expectations are priced in. Negative values mean the company is expected to report a loss.
TrendScore (0–100)
How strong the stock's current price momentum is. 70+ means it is in a healthy uptrend and trading above its key moving averages. Below 40 suggests weakness or a downtrend.
RSI (14)
Short-term momentum indicator — not a measure of company quality. Helps identify potentially overbought or oversold conditions. Under 30 = Oversold (contrarian watch); 30–50 = Weak; 50–70 = Healthy; Over 70 = Extended (proceed carefully).
Stock 1M %
How much the stock price changed over the past month. Green = gained; red = fell. Context matters — a drop in a strong stock may be a buying opportunity; a drop in a weak stock may signal further decline.
vs S&P 500 1M
How this stock performed compared to the S&P 500 index over the past month. Positive (green) means it outperformed the index; negative (red) means it lagged.
Below 52W High
How far the current price is below its 52-week high. 0–10% means the stock is near its peak. A larger pullback can mean opportunity — or that something is wrong. Best interpreted alongside Quality and Risk.
Risk
The current risk level for this stock. SAFE = stable, healthy trend; WATCH = some elevated risk, proceed carefully; RISKY = high volatility or technical breakdown; AVOID = not suitable right now.
Earnings In
Days until the next earnings report. Stocks with earnings due soon carry higher short-term risk. Negative values mean earnings already passed this cycle.
Risk Flags
CLEAR = no active flags. HIGH_BETA = moves sharply with the market; BELOW_SMA50 = below 50-day average; EARNINGS_NEAR = report within ~3 weeks; LOW_QUALITY = weak fundamentals.
